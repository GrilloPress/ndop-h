Whenever you visit an NHS service, care professionals must keep records about you. These may be electronic, paper or a mixture of both. Your records contain your health and care information.

You can use our online service to request that your personally identifiable data is not used or shared beyond your own individual care.

Who uses your data

The NHS collects health and care data from a wide variety of NHS trusts, local authorities and independent sector groups.

Research bodies and organisations can request access to this data.

They must meet strict conditions and criteria in order to be considered.

Third party marketing or insurance companies will not be given access to your data, unless you specifically request us to do so.

How data is kept secure

The benefits of data sharing

The NHS may share this personally identifiable information in two different ways. The first reason for sharing is for your own individual care. This allows healthcare professionals, like doctors and dentists, to see your important medical details.

The second reason for sharing is to help the NHS research, plan and improve health and social care services for you and your family.

This helps the NHS:

  • plan essential facilities like heart units
  • understand the need for specialist care wards
  • research potential cures for life-threatening diseases
  • improve treatment for serious illnesses
Find out more

How opting out works

If you opt-out of sharing your personally identifiable data, details like your name and address will no longer appear in data shared by the NHS.

This will only happen if opt-outs apply to the data set being shared.

Once an opt-out request is received, it can take up to 21 days before it can be applied to future data releases.

Find out more

Register your preference

By sharing your data you can contribute to the health of future generations. However, if you don't agree with using your data for research and/or planning purposes, you can opt-out of this.

The decision you make will not affect your individual care.
