The NHS collects health and care data from a wide variety of NHS trusts, local authorities and independent health care sector groups. Research bodies and organisations can request access to this data. They must meet strict conditions and criteria in order to be considered.

Organisations that can request access to data may include:

  • university researchers
  • hospital researchers
  • royal colleges

How your data is used

✔

Personally identifiable data will only be released if it is:

  • used to support the delivery of health and social care services
  • required by law
✖

Access to personally identifiable data will not be given to:

  • third party marketing companies
  • third party insurance companies

(unless you specifically request us to do so).

Before your data is used

A signed data sharing agreement must be in place between the NHS and the organisation requesting information. This legally protects the data and states the rules on what it can be used for.

An agreement for data sharing will include:

  • the type of data being shared
  • the purpose for sharing data
  • all other data security conditions
